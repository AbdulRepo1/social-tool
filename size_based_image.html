How to Compress an Image to a Specific KB or MB

Reducing an image's file size to an exact kilobyte or megabyte limit is required by many platforms — email attachments, profile photo uploads, e-commerce product images, and government/form portals. This tool makes it effortless.

  1. Upload your image — drag and drop or click to browse. JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF and BMP are all accepted.
  2. Set your target size — type the maximum file size you need (e.g. 150) and choose KB or MB from the dropdown.
  3. Choose output format — JPEG for broadest compatibility, WebP for smaller files on modern browsers.
  4. Click Compress Image — the binary-search algorithm runs entirely in your browser in seconds.
  5. Review the comparison — see original vs optimized side-by-side with file size, dimensions and quality level.
  6. Download — click the green button to save the optimized file instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about compressing images to a specific KB or MB size.

How do I compress an image to a specific KB size?

Upload your image, type your target size (e.g. 150 KB) in the input field, select KB from the dropdown, and click Compress Image. The tool runs a smart binary-search loop that automatically finds the best quality setting to land under your target.

Will the compressed image look different?

The algorithm always tries the highest possible quality first and works downward only as much as needed. For most targets above 50 KB, quality loss is imperceptible. If dimensions must be reduced, the aspect ratio is always preserved to avoid distortion.

How does the binary-search algorithm work?

The tool starts with quality at 1.0 (maximum) and 0.1 (minimum). It encodes the image at the midpoint (0.55), checks whether the output fits the target size, then narrows the range — raising the floor if it fits, lowering the ceiling if it doesn't — repeating until the range is less than 0.02. This guarantees the maximum possible quality in under 20 iterations.

What is the proportional downscaling fallback?

If even the lowest quality setting (0.1) cannot hit the target size, the tool reduces the image dimensions by 5% (maintaining the original aspect ratio) and runs the binary-search quality loop again. It repeats this until the target is met or dimensions reach 10% of the original.

Is this tool really free with no sign-up?

Yes, completely free and no account required. All compression happens in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Your images are never transmitted to any server.

Can I compress PNG to a specific KB?

Yes. Upload your PNG and select JPEG or WebP as the output format. PNG itself is lossless and often cannot be compressed to small sizes, but converting to JPEG or WebP while targeting a specific size works extremely well.
